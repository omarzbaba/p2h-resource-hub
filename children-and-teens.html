CENTRAL AUTOPSY SERVICE

For Children & Teens: SandCastles

A safe, caring place for grieving children, teens, and their families.
⤓ Download this guide (PDF)

Children grieve differently than adults, in bursts, through play, and with big questions. SandCastles is Henry Ford’s grief support program created to give children and teens, and the adults who love them, a place to grieve together and know they are not alone.

What SandCastles offers

  • Year-round peer support groups for ages 3–18, in person and virtual
  • Groups that gather children close in age to share, learn, and heal through creative expression, games, and mindfulness
  • An annual overnight summer camp for grieving children and teens
  • Education and support for schools and community organizations

Services reach families across Wayne, Oakland, and Macomb counties and beyond.

Connect with SandCastles

Call 313-771-7005 to enroll, or email sandcastles@hfhs.org with questions. Learn more at aboutsandcastles.org.

Helping a grieving child

  • Use simple, honest words like “died” rather than “passed away” or “lost,” which can confuse younger children.
  • Let them ask the same questions more than once; repetition is how children make sense of loss.
  • Keep routines where you can, predictability feels safe.
  • Let them see your feelings; it gives them permission to have their own.

More children’s grief resources
