QUESTIONS

Frequently asked

Does an autopsy delay funeral arrangements?
In most cases, no. An autopsy usually does not significantly delay funeral plans, and it does not prevent a viewing. Your loved one is treated with care, dignity and respect at every step.
When is the medical examiner involved?
Michigan law requires certain deaths (for example accidental, sudden, or unexpected deaths) to be reported to the county medical examiner, who decides whether an autopsy is required. See Understanding Autopsy for details.
How long do results take?
A final report often takes several weeks, and sometimes longer. We keep you informed and explain the findings in plain language when they are ready.
Can we still have a viewing or open casket?
Yes. An autopsy is performed with great care and does not prevent an open-casket viewing.
How do I request my loved one’s records?
Records are released to the estate’s executor with a signed authorization and a letter of authority from the Probate Court. See Medical Records & Death Certificates.
